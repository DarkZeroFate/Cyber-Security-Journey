# Cyber-Security-Journey
*Transitioning from Business Management to Cybersecurity*

Hi! I'm 24 years old, and I come from a Business Management background. I'm currently on a mission to pivot into the world of Cybersecurity.

To be honest, this hasn't been easy. I’m balancing a demanding 10-hour work schedule, studying for civil service exams (CPNS), and trying to learn Cybersecurity from scratch. This repository is my personal scratchpad—a place to dump my notes, track my home lab progress, and document the messy bits where I inevitably break my virtual machines.

### Why does this exist?
I’ve realized that just reading about security isn't enough. I'm building this to prove to myself—and hopefully, future hiring teams—that I’m a hands-on learner. I’m not here to build a collection of "perfect" tutorials. I’m here to build things, break them, get frustrated, and then fix them.

### What’s in here?
This isn't a professional wiki; it's my genuine learning journey:
- **[Network Lab](./network-lab/):** My home lab setups (Kali Linux & Ubuntu). I document my configurations here, along with a "Troubleshooting Log" for whenever I hit a wall.
- **[Projects](./projects/):** Small, hands-on experiments (like malware traffic analysis or testing reverse shells).
- **[Notes](./notes/):** Random technical snippets on tools like Splunk or Sysmon that I’m exploring so I don’t forget the details later.

### A note for visitors
If you're starting from scratch too, feel free to look around. Since I'm learning by doing, you'll see things that are definitely "work-in-progress." 

I’m always open to feedback. If you spot a configuration I’ve messed up or a security practice I could improve, please let me know. I’m here to learn and grow.
